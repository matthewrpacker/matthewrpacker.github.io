My goal is to provide some guidance to people interested in prototyping their new app idea. Throughout this post, I will identify useful tactics, concepts, and resources. This is in no way comprehensive, but hopefully it gets you started. Below are some of the topics that I will cover.

  • Why Prototyping is Important
  • Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
  • Testing (Alpha and Beta)
  • Business Model Canvas

Why Prototyping is Important

Prototyping allows developers to bring their idea to life. Many times, people hesitate to release a usable version of their idea until everything is perfect. However, developing a usable product or service is the only way for us to learn. If you have a spare hour, and I highly recommend the Fake It Till You Make It talk. This talk discusses and demonstrates Apple's prototyping process, and took place in 2014 at the Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC). You may need to use Safari to view the video...

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

The MVP is usually the first iteration of a new product. Basically, the MVP answers this question: How can we learn the most about our product, and customers, with the least amount of effort? The MVP gives us a chance to prove our value proposition. Sometimes people have found a solution to a problem that no one has. The MVP helps us avoid going down that path too long. Take your original idea, with all of its features, and refactor it to the most basic form. Although we are aiming for simplicity, it is important that the MVP still communicates the intended value of the product.

Testing (Alpha and Beta)

Now that we have our MVP, we want to test it. Testing allows us to learn more about the product and the people that use the product. After gaining feedback from our testing, we restart the cycle; Prototype - MVP - Testing. Maybe we learned that no one understands our value proposition, or possibly everyone mentioned that "this" feature would make the product so much better. Without testing, we can't benefit from user feedback and move forward with confidence.

Testing can be split into two groups, Alpha and Beta. You can think of Alpha as internal testing and Beta as external testing. Commonly, Alpha testing is performed by people internal to the company (e.g. engineers, designers, and managers). This gives us the benefit of feedback in a controlled environment. Beta testing is usually done after we have gone through many iterations of the product development, and feel that we are ready to introduce it to the public.

Business Model Canvas

The Business Model Canvas (BMC) is a framework used for developing a new product/service/business and for analyzing existing products/services/ businesses. The framework helps people flesh out their initial concept and develop a workable business model. The framework contains nine boxes: Key Partners, Key Activities, Key Resources, Value Propositions, Customer Relationships, Channels, Customer Segments, Cost Structure, and Revenue Streams. I found the best way to use this framework is to simply draw it out on a whiteboard and add post-it notes to each of the boxes. This framework should help you analyze your product or service from different perspectives.
